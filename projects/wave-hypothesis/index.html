The Wave Hypothesis is an Active Inference Institute research thread that investigates wave-based framings of dynamics relevant to Active Inference and theoretical neurobiology. It is an open, exploratory line of inquiry rather than a settled result: it sits at the intersection of dynamical-systems thinking, theoretical neurobiology, and Active Inference. The full hypothesis statement and supporting materials are maintained on a dedicated public hub, and the work is ongoing — carried in the spirit of the Institute's motto, Act. Infer. Serve.

Overview

The Wave Hypothesis asks how wave-based framings of dynamics might illuminate questions in Active Inference and theoretical neurobiology. Rather than asserting fixed conclusions, the thread organizes a developing line of inquiry — drawing connections between wave-oriented descriptions of change and the modeling traditions of Active Inference. The specific scientific claims, formulations, and supporting arguments are maintained on the dedicated hub, where they can be discussed and revised as the work matures. Treating it as a living research question, rather than a finished answer, is part of the point.

Wave Hypothesis shortlinkResearch

Where it sits

The thread lives at the meeting point of three traditions. Dynamical-systems thinking offers a vocabulary for how states evolve over time — trajectories, oscillations, stability, and the propagation of change. Theoretical neurobiology asks how the structure and activity of nervous systems can be described and explained at multiple scales. Active Inference provides a formal framing in which perception, action, and learning are understood as a single process of minimizing prediction error under a generative model. The Wave Hypothesis explores what becomes visible when wave-based descriptions of dynamics are brought into conversation with these framings — without committing in advance to any one formulation.

How it fits the program

The Wave Hypothesis is carried as a research thread within the Institute's ReInference Unit, the research arm of a participatory, open-science 501(c)(3) nonprofit organized around three units: EduActive for education, ReInference for research, and Administrative for operations. ReInference supports research projects and theoretical inquiry across the Active Inference Ecosystem, and this thread connects to the Institute's broader catalog of projects. As open science, the work is meant to be examined, reused, and improved in public rather than held privately.

Why it matters

Bringing a new descriptive lens to familiar dynamics is one of the ways a research program renews itself. If wave-based framings turn out to clarify how adaptive systems represent and respond to change, they could enrich both the conceptual and the formal sides of Active Inference. Because the thread is exploratory, its value at this stage is in sharpening questions, testing framings against existing theory, and surfacing where wave-oriented descriptions help or hinder. The honest framing — what is genuinely open versus what is established elsewhere — is kept on the hub.

Participate

Because the hypothesis is still developing, participation centers on reading the current materials, following the discussion, and contributing perspectives or critiques. Newcomers can begin with the materials on the hub to understand the framing and its open questions. Those who want grounding in the underlying framework can start with the Institute's Active Inference resources, then connect through its general participation pathways to engage with research threads like this one. Contributions from people with backgrounds in dynamics, neurobiology, mathematics, and Active Inference are all welcome.
