The Institute's strategy is organized around its motto — Act. Infer. Serve. — and its mission to advance Active Inference as a scientific, educational, and applied practice. Strategy is enacted through three program units and through open-source, participatory infrastructure and ecosystem partnerships, with the specific current priorities maintained on the strategy hub.

Mission and Motto

The Institute's strategy follows directly from its mission: to advance Active Inference as a scientific, educational, and applied practice; to strengthen cognitive security and healthy information ecosystems; to make research more reproducible and accessible; and to teach and mentor broadly, including under-served communities. The motto — Act. Infer. Serve. — names the orientation that holds these aims together: acting in the world, inferring through rigorous modeling and study, and serving the communities and ecosystems the Institute works with.

About the Institute

Enacted Through Three Program Units

As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, the Institute carries out its strategy through three program units: EduActive for education, ReInference for research, and Administrative for operations. This division lets strategic aims become concrete programs — educational pathways, research activity, and the operational backbone that supports both — while keeping the work coordinated under a single mission. Each unit translates the broad strategic direction into the specific activities it is responsible for.

Open-Source and Participatory by Design

Strategy is enacted not only through internal programs but through open-source, participatory infrastructure and partnerships across the broader Active Inference Ecosystem. The Institute treats reproducibility, accessibility, and open participation as strategic commitments rather than afterthoughts: materials are public, contribution pathways are open, and progress depends on collaboration with researchers, learners, and partner organizations. This approach lets the strategy compound through shared, reusable work.
