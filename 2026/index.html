This is the Institute's annual overview for 2026 — a single entry point to the year's planned activities, programs, and overall direction. It gathers work across the Institute's three program units into one place so that members, collaborators, and visitors can see how the year fits together. The detailed plan, goals, milestones, and event calendar are maintained on the 2026 hub and are being migrated onto this site.

What this page covers

This annual overview consolidates the year's direction for the Active Inference Institute, a participatory, open-science 501(c)(3) nonprofit guided by its motto, Act. Infer. Serve. Rather than tracking a single project, it offers a year-level view: the kinds of activities planned, the program units carrying them out, and how the year's work connects to the Institute's broader mission. Specific goals, milestones, and dates are not asserted here — they are maintained on the 2026 hub linked at the foot of this page.

About the Institute

Programs across the year

The Institute's work in 2026 is carried out through its three program units. EduActive leads education-oriented activities such as learning groups and course development. ReInference leads research-oriented activities and projects. The Administrative unit supports operations, governance, and coordination throughout the year. Together these units organize the planned activities reflected in this overview, and the structure page describes how they fit within the wider organization.

Activities and direction

A year-level view is most useful as a way to see across the activities that would otherwise be scattered. The activities page gathers the recurring sessions, working groups, and public events that make up the Institute's ongoing rhythm, while the projects page shows the concrete lines of work those activities support. Reading them alongside this overview gives a sense of where the year's energy is directed without committing to specifics that are still being finalized on the hub.

Participate in 2026

As a participatory, open-science organization, the Institute invites contributors to take part in the year's work. Ways to engage span learning, research, and operational support across the three program units, and most participation begins with a single step into an existing activity or group. The get-involved page is the best starting point for finding current opportunities, and the learning resources offer an on-ramp for anyone new to Active Inference who wants context before joining.

Stay current with the calendar and ecosystem

The calendar maintains the schedule of public sessions and events alongside the annual plan, and it is the most reliable way to track what is happening week to week as the year unfolds. Much of the Institute's 2026 work also connects outward to a broader ecosystem of collaborators and aligned organizations, so the ecosystem page is worth reading next for a view of the partnerships and shared efforts that the year's activities touch.
