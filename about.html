The Active Inference Institute is a participatory open science institute dedicated to improving the accessibility, rigor, and applicability of the Active Inference framework.

Purpose

AII supports education, research, training, and applications related to Active Inference. The Institute welcomes different backgrounds, time zones, and familiarity levels, and organizes the ecosystem through programs, projects, public media, research resources, and governance structures.

Institute and ecosystem living documentResearchDiscord inviteCommunity

Mission, Vision, Values, and Principles

The Institute emphasizes integrity, open learning, responsible conduct, professional development, research excellence, and practical service. The public site presents these as an operating stance: make Active Inference more accessible, rigorous, and applicable while supporting ethical collaboration.

Institute Structure

The Institute is represented through a Board of Directors, Officers, a Scientific Advisory Board, Members, organizational units, project groups, learning groups, and public communication channels. The structure supports stable stewardship and flexible participation.

Information Surfaces

Public channels include Discord, Coda-based resources, newsletters, video and podcast channels, social profiles, project repositories, and GitHub project pages. This website organizes those surfaces into stable visitor pathways.

act_inf_metaanalysis

A computational meta-analysis of the Active Inference literature. An automated LLM pipeline extracts assertions from 849 papers across theory, tools, and domains, structuring them as nanopublications. By mapping these into a citation-weighted knowledge graph, this project provides a reproducible, living review of hypothesis-level consensus.
