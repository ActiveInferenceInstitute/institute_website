2026 is the Institute's ongoing year — a light entry point to the various things happening across the organization. The best places to follow along are the Activities page and the Calendar, where recurring sessions, working groups, and public events appear as they are scheduled. The fuller record is maintained on the 2026 hub.

Following along in 2026

Rather than fixing the year's plan in advance, this page points to where the Institute's 2026 activities actually show up as they happen. The Activities page gathers the recurring sessions, working groups, and public events that make up the Institute's ongoing rhythm, and the Calendar is the most reliable way to see what is happening week to week. For how this year follows from the Institute's longer story, see the History page.

Programs carrying the work

The year's work runs through the Institute's three program units: EduActive for education, ReInference for research, and the Administrative unit for operations and coordination. The structure page describes how they fit together.

Institute structureEduActiveReInference

Take part

As a participatory, open-science organization, the Institute invites contributors to join the year's work — across learning, research, and operational support. Most participation begins with a single step into an existing activity or group; the get-involved page is the best starting point, and the learning resources offer an on-ramp for anyone new to Active Inference.

Visitors seeking an institutional orientation. About the Institute

The Active Inference Institute is a volunteer-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to improving the accessibility, rigor, and applicability of Active Inference. We welcome people of all backgrounds, time zones, and familiarity levels.

 Participants looking for synchronous ways to learn and contribute. Activities

Activities are the Institute's live participation surface: learning groups, model streams, project meetings, public media, and recurring updates.

 Visitors seeking to understand how the Institute is organized and governed. Institute Structure

The Active Inference Institute is organized around a governing Board of Directors, executive Officers, a Scientific Advisory Board, and two operational units for education and research.
