Institute programs turn interest into participation through volunteer, internship, mentorship, fellowship, partnership, philanthropy, grants, and open source pathways.

Source coverage: Pages 58-85. Browse the page-by-page Source Atlas.

Volunteer

Volunteering is the broadest on-ramp for participation. The source describes active and documented participation in learning groups and projects, with room for people to contribute according to available time, background, and skills.

Discord invite Community Weekly updates Updates

Internship and Mentorship

Internships and mentorship provide more structured engagement. These pathways help participants learn by contributing to projects, public artifacts, educational materials, research workflows, and community operations.

Fellows

The Fellowship pathway supports contributors who provide regular progress reports and develop research, applications, or public resources aligned with the Institute and ecosystem.

Partnership and Philanthropy

Partners and donors support shared research, public education, infrastructure, and project development. The PDF frames partnership as a practical exchange of time, attention, expertise, funding, and institutional support.

Open Source

Open source is treated as an ecosystem support function: publish reusable work, improve project infrastructure, and make shared technical resources discoverable.
