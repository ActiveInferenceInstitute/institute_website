Institute programs turn interest into participation through volunteer, internship, mentorship, fellowship, partnership, philanthropy, grants, and open-source pathways.

Volunteer

Volunteering is the broadest on-ramp for participation. People can contribute to learning groups, projects, operations, writing, analysis, media, and community support according to available time, background, and skills.

Discord inviteCommunityWeekly updatesCommunity

Internship and Mentorship

Internships and mentorship provide more structured engagement. These pathways help participants learn by contributing to projects, public materials, educational work, research workflows, and community operations.

Fellows

The Fellowship pathway supports contributors who provide regular progress reports and develop research, applications, or public resources aligned with the Institute and ecosystem.

Partnership and Philanthropy

Partners and donors support shared research, public education, infrastructure, and project development. Partnership is a practical exchange of time, attention, expertise, funding, and institutional support.

Open Source

Open source is an ecosystem support function: publish reusable work, improve project infrastructure, and make shared technical resources discoverable.
